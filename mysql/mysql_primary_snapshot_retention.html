Primary snapshot retention for MySQL

Primary snapshot retention for MySQL instances provides data protection and recoverability by making it possible to set various retention policies. These policies include hourly, daily, monthly, and yearly retention of snapshots to meet compliance, business continuity, and disaster recovery requirements.

Primary snapshot retention refers to the length of time RSC retains the primary snapshots of MySQL instances. Configuring a primary retention policy protects a MySQL instance and makes it recoverable according to your requirements.

You can set various retention policies to meet compliance, business continuity, and disaster recovery requirements.
The following table describes the default snapshot types available, along with their retention periods and use cases.
Snapshot type Retention period Use case
Hourly 24 to 72 hours High-change environments: Captures frequent changes for applications with high transaction rates.
Daily 14 to 30 days Operational recovery: Quick recovery of recent changes due to accidental deletions or corruption.
Weekly snapshots 1 to 6 months Mid-term recovery: Balances frequent recovery needs with storage efficiency for less critical data.
Monthly 12 to 36 months Regulatory compliance: Meets legal or industry requirements for data retention and audits.
Quarterly snapshots 1 to 5 years Business continuity: Ensures prolonged data retention for quarterly reviews and audits.
Yearly More than 3 years Long-term archiving: Preserves critical data for historical reference or long-term compliance needs.
