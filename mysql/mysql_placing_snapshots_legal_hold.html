Placing a MySQL snapshot on legal hold

Placing a legal hold on a MySQL snapshot indefinitely retains that snapshot regardless of the assigned SLA Domain retention policy.

About this task

Perform this task to protect specific MySQL snapshots from expiration due to legal requirements.

If an SLA Domain specifies an archival location, the archived snapshot is placed on legal hold by default. If no archival location is specified, the local snapshot is placed on legal hold.

Procedure

  1. Log in to RSC.
  2. Click and select Data Protection.
    The Data Protection dashboard appears.
  3. On the navigation bar, click Inventory.
    The Inventory page appears.
  4. Click the MySQL inventory card.
    The MySQL page appears.
  5. Click the name of the MySQL instance containing the snapshots you want to place on legal hold.
  6. Click Snapshots.
    The list of MySQL snapshots appears.
  7. Click for a snapshot and select Place on Legal Hold.
    The Place Legal Hold on Snapshot dialog box appears.
  8. Optional: Clear the I want to retain the local snapshot until legal hold is removed check box if you do not want to retain the local snapshot until the legal hold is removed.
  9. Optional: In Add Note, add a reason for placing the snapshot on legal hold.
  10. Click Confirm.

Results

RSC places the selected snapshot on legal hold.

Snapshots on legal hold display . When you hover over the icon, a message appears confirming that the legal hold is in place.
