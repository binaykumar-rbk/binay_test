Legal hold limitations

In RSC, the legal hold feature has some limitations.

Only administrators can perform legal hold operations.

Legal hold does not transfer from the source Rubrik cluster to the target Rubrik cluster and it also does not transfer in the other direction.

The following are key points about the handling of legal hold information and snapshot expiration between source and target Rubrik clusters:
  • When snapshots of a protected object replicate to a target Rubrik cluster, and the snapshots of that protected object are placed on legal hold on the source Rubrik cluster, the corresponding snapshots on the target Rubrik cluster are not automatically placed on legal hold.
  • Snapshots on the target Rubrik cluster expire according to the SLA Domain policy.
  • When a replicated snapshot is placed under legal hold on the target Rubrik cluster, the corresponding snapshot on the source Rubrik cluster is not automatically placed under legal hold.
  • Snapshots on the source Rubrik cluster expire according to the SLA Domain policy.
The snapshots for the following workloads cannot be placed on legal hold:
  • Log backups for SQL Server databases
  • Oracle databases
  • Managed Volumes
  • Direct archive filesets

You can place the replicated snapshots from the target Rubrik cluster on legal hold. However, the replicated snapshots from the source Rubrik cluster cannot be placed on legal hold.

Related concepts
  • Legal hold for snapshot retention
    Legal hold ensures that snapshots are retained indefinitely until the hold is removed, overriding the scheduled expiry of an SLA Domain policy. This ensures that snapshots are available as long as necessary to meet legal obligations.
