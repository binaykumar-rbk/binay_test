Immutable Rubrik Cloud Vault locations

Rubrik Cloud Vault locations provide immutability for cloud-native, NAS CD, and data center workloads, depending on the Rubrik CDM version and the cloud provider. Immutability ensures retention policies are enforced and prevents alteration or deletion of snapshots for the specified period, safeguarding data integrity.

Rubrik Cloud Vault (RCV) locations created for cloud-native and NAS CD workloads are immutable by default. For data center workloads, immutability support for RCV location depends on the Rubrik cluster version.

Immutable RCV locations on Azure

RCV locations created for Rubrik clusters running Rubrik CDM versions 9.1 and later support blob-level immutability and do not require manual configuration of immutability settings at the time of creating the location in RSC. The snapshots archived with blob-level immutability are immutable for the retention period specified in the SLA Domain.

RCV locations created for Rubrik clusters running Rubrik CDM versions earlier than version 9.1 leverage the container-level immutability feature of Azure storage containers and require manual configuration of immutability settings at the time of creating the location in RSC.

You can enable container-level immutability for RCV locations only when creating the location. Snapshots that a Rubrik cluster writes to an immutable container cannot be altered or deleted for the retention period specified in the SLA Domain assigned to that protected object.

RCV locations with container-level immutability do not support archival consolidation, smart tiering, or early snapshot expiration. After the initial configuration, you cannot shorten the immutability lock period.

Immutable RCV locations on AWS

RCV locations created for Rubrik clusters running Rubrik CDM versions 9.3.1 and later support object-level immutability and do not require manual configuration of immutability settings at the time of creating the location in RSC. The snapshots archived with object-level immutability are immutable for the retention period specified in the SLA Domain.

Immutable RCV locations on GCP

RCV locations on GCP support object-level immutability, and it is always on. You do not need to manually configure object-level immutability at the time of creating the location in RSC. The archived snapshots are immutable for the retention period specified in the SLA Domain.

Immutability lock period calculation

RCV locations with container-level or bucket-level immutability use a calculated value called the Immutability Lock Period.

The formula to calculate the immutability lock period is as follows:
ILP = SLA RP - AT + FUA
Where:
  • ILP stands for Immutability Lock Period. It is the calculated length of time the objects in the RCV location are immutably stored.
  • SLA RP stands for SLA Retention Period. It is the retention period specified by the SLA Domain.
  • AT stands for Archival Threshold. It is the local retention period specified by the SLA Domain. This is the time that snapshots are stored on the Rubrik cluster before archiving.
  • FUA stands for Full Upload Adjustment. It is the number of days between full backup uploads.
    The value of the FUA varies within a range that is determined by the snapshot frequency. The ranges are listed in the following table:
    SLA Domain frequency Full upload range
    Hourly 15 to 30 days
    Daily 60 to 90 days

Immutability lock period calculation for hourly snapshots

Suppose you have an SLA Domain with the following parameters:
  • SLA RP: 180 days
  • AT: 30 days
  • FUA: Fixed at 20 days
Using the immutability lock period formula, you can calculate the ILP as follows: 
ILP = 180 − 30 + 20 = 170 days
In this example, the data is immutably stored for 170 days.

The immutability lock period of a snapshot at an immutable RCV location is based on the retention period of the SLA Domain assigned to that snapshot and increases when the Rubrik cluster writes new snapshots of the source data to the location. This increase of retention period ensures the continuity of the snapshot chain. Due to the immutable nature of the storage, storage usage and related charges can increase beyond the expectations set by mutable storage usage patterns.

RCV locations with immutable storage only support snapshots protected by an SLA Domain that takes snapshots at a single frequency and with an archival policy enabled.
Note:
For specific questions about configuring immutability, contact your Rubrik account team for guidance. After immutability is configured, you cannot reverse it. Snapshots stored in an immutable RCV location cannot be deleted before the immutability lock period expires.
