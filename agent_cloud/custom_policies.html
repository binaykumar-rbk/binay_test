Custom policies

Custom policies allow you to extend Agent Cloud governance with site-specific rules that the system policies do not cover. You write these policies using natural language, and they are evaluated by an AI judge.

After enabling system policies, create custom policies to cover governance requirements that the built-in policies do not address — requirements specific to your organization, your data, or your industry. A custom policy is most effective when it targets a specific scenario: preventing agents from sending proprietary research to external services through tool calls, blocking a combination of tools considered risky when used together, or enforcing a site-specific approval rule before agents can take a particular action.

The Agent Cloud system policies cover a wide range of common security, compliance, and operational concerns. However, every organization has its own requirements that depend on the data, workflows, and tools that AI agents interact with in your environment.

Custom policies allow you to define these rules by describing the intent of the policy and letting Agent Cloud generate the underlying policy structure. You write custom policies in natural language, without using any code.

Important:
You can create custom policies that use either Monitoring or Monitoring and Blocking enforcement. However, when you create your custom, when you create your custom policies, keep in mind that Agent Cloud receives tool call information only when it makes a blocking decision, and it can block only tool calls. Custom policies that depend on either of these capabilities might be less effective than the system policies.

How custom policies work

Each custom policy is evaluated by an AI judge, which is a specialized model that reviews the context and intent of an AI agent interaction against the natural language description you provided.

When an AI agent performs a tool call that matches the conditions of the policy, the AI judge classifies the interaction as either safe or a violation. Agent Cloud records each violation on the Activity page. From the Activity page you can review the action, the AI agent that performed it, and the reasoning the AI judge applied.

You create custom policies from the Policies page using the Create Policy wizard. During creation, the wizard rates the strength of your policy across four characteristics: goals and instructions, key definitions, behaviors, and reference examples. The wizard provides recommendations to help you refine the policy before you save it. You can also validate the policy against sample input before deployment to confirm that it produces the classifications you expect.

When to use custom policies

Custom policies cover governance requirements that the Agent Cloud system policies do not address, including requirements that are specific to your site. Common examples include:

  • Restricting which tools an AI agent can call when it interacts with a particular data source, customer, or environment.
  • Preventing AI agents from sending categories of internal data, such as proprietary research, partner contracts, or non-public financial figures, to external services through tool calls.
  • Enforcing site-specific approval, disclosure, or escalation rules that apply only to your organization or industry.
  • Detecting actions that combine otherwise acceptable tools in a way that your organization considers risky.

Limitations

Custom policies have the following limitations:

  • Custom policies rely on an AI judge to classify each interaction. The accuracy of the policy depends on the quality of the natural language description, the definitions you provide, the boundaries you set, and the reference examples you include. The Create Policy wizard provides a strength rating and recommendations to help you write a policy that produces accurate classifications.
  • Custom policies do not replace the system policies. The system policies cover broad security, compliance, and operational risks. Custom policies cover governance requirements that are specific to your site.
  • The AI judge classifies the examples you provide against the sample input. This validation does not guarantee the same classification for every interaction the policy evaluates in production.
