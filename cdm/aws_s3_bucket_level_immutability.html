Bucket-level immutability for Amazon S3

Amazon S3 archival locations on Rubrik clusters running Rubrik CDM versions earlier than 9.3.1 can use bucket-level immutability of Amazon S3 storage.

When configuring an Amazon S3 archival location on Rubrik clusters running Rubrik CDM versions earlier than 9.3.1, you can access Advanced settings in the Create Archival Location wizard to enable bucket-level immutability. For Rubrik clusters running Rubrik CDM versions 9.3.1 and later, you cannot configure bucket-level immutability. Bucket-level immutability keeps snapshots in the snapshot chain immutable for the immutability lock period you specify when configuring the archival location. The snapshot chain limit is the number of incremental snapshots that build on a given full snapshot. The Rubrik cluster takes a new full snapshot after reaching the snapshot chain limit.

Consider the following scenarios for a workload assigned an SLA Domain that takes monthly snapshots, has a retention period of two years, and has a snapshot chain limit of 60 snapshots:
  • Configuring a non-immutable archival location results in a snapshot chain that spans five years of activity. Snapshots expire after a two-year retention period. However, the archival location contents are not subject to deletion, garbage collection, or space reclamation until all snapshots in the chain expire. For a non-immutable archival location, a two-year retention period and a five-year snapshot chain length result in the oldest snapshot in the chain being seven years old when the chain expires.

  • Configuring an immutable archival location with a longer lock period results in the last snapshot in a chain being taken in the last month of the fifth year. To retain the last snapshot for two years according to the retention period specified in the SLA Domain, you need to set the immutability lock period to seven years.

    The immutability lock on the Amazon S3 bucket also preserves the last snapshot in the chain taken at the end of the fifth year for the entire seven years. The snapshot chain is effectively locked for a total of 12 years.

    This results in an unalterable 12-year period of data retention and associated charges. Due to immutability requirements, the entire chain is preserved for 12 years, compared to seven years for a non-immutable archival location.

  • Configuring an immutable archival location with a shorter immutability lock period results in more frequent full uploads.

    Reducing the immutability lock period to three years locks the first snapshot for only three years. As the last snapshot in the chain has to be retained for two years based on the retention period specified in the SLA Domain, the last snapshot cannot be older than one year to ensure immutability. This means that the snapshot chain length for monthly uploads will be 12 snapshots, and a full upload is taken once every 12 months. In this case, the last snapshot in the chain is locked for the immutability lock period of three years.

    The entire chain is preserved for four years for an immutable archival location with a shorter immutability lock period compared to 12 years for an immutable archival location with a longer immutability lock period. However, a shorter chain of 12 months compared to 60 months requires more frequent full uploads.

Bucket-level immutability keeps snapshots immutable for the immutability lock period, which you can calculate based on factors like the SLA Domain frequency and archival retention. The immutability lock period is higher than the SLA Domain retention period in the archive because the snapshot chains require supporting incremental snapshots. As a result, enabling immutability increases storage costs due to the longer retention of snapshots. Shorter immutability lock periods result in more frequent full snapshots.
