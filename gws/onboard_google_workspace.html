Onboard a Google Workspace

Onboard a Google Workspace to connect your Google Workspace organization to RSC to enable data protection for your Google Workspace workloads, including Gmail and Google Drive.

This initial setup establishes a secure and authorized connection that enables RSC to discover your data and perform backup and recovery operations. The process uses an OAuth-based workflow along with Domain-Wide Delegation (DWD), a standard method for granting service account permissions across an entire domain.

Onboarding scenarios

  • Greenfield setup: RSC does not yet protect the Google Workspace organization.
  • Brownfield setup: RSC protects an existing organization for other workloads. You can add protection for a new workload, such as Gmail or Google Drive, to these organizations.

Configuration choices

During onboarding, you define how RSC interacts with your Google data by making several key choices:

  • Application selection: Selecting Gmail, Google Drive, or both to protect those workloads.
  • Default SLA Domain: Assigning a baseline protection policy to the entire organization.
  • GCP region: Selecting the specific GCP region where RSC stores your backup data.
  • Spam exclusion for Gmail: Selecting to exclude the Spam label from backups to optimize storage efficiency and focus on important business data.

Multi-geo location

RSC stores snapshots in the same geographical region as the source data to support compliance with data residency regulations. If you enable multi-geo support while onboarding your workload with RSC, you can select specific GCP regions within the geographical region defined in your Google Workspace Admin Console to store your snapshots.

Discovery and synchronization

After you onboard your workload with RSC, an initial data synchronization begins. During this phase, RSC discovers all protectable workloads, including:

  • My Drive and Shared Drive contents.
  • User Mailboxes and their associated labels.
  • Organizational Units (OUs) for hierarchical management.
