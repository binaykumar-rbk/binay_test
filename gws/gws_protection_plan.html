Plan your Google Workspace protection

Before setting up a Google Workspace organization in RSC, you must plan the user and access management for designated RSC users. This planning also involves reviewing the permissions that RSC requires in your Google Workspace environment to protect your workloads.

Planning involves the following key activities:

  • Establishing a framework for user access management: You can categorize your RSC users based on their roles and responsibilities, and assign appropriate privileges through custom roles to limit access according to the principle of least privilege.
  • Reviewing required permissions: You can review the specific API permissions that RSC requires to protect your Google Workspace workloads. Understanding these permissions provides clarity on the operations RSC performs, such as near real-time snapshots of user data and metadata. For more information, see Requirements for protecting Google Workspace.
  • Understanding service account requirements: RSC requires a service account with delegated domain-wide authority to access user data.

Understanding these permissions clarifies the operations that RSC performs to protect your data, such as:

  • Discovery: Viewing user accounts, groups, and organizational structures.
  • Backup: Performing near real-time snapshots of user data, conversation threads, and metadata.
  • Recovery: Restoring Google Drive files and folders, or Gmail emails and threads, to the original or an alternate location.
