    • Quorum authorization

      Quorum authorization is a security feature that enforces the requirement of getting additional approvals before a requester can perform data-modifying actions in RSC.

      • Key actors and responsibilities in quorum authorization

        Understanding the roles and responsibilities of key actors in quorum authorization management is important to ensure a secure and effective system.

      • Quorum authorization policy

        A quorum authorization policy is the means by which quorum authorization is enforced. To ensure that a single user does not run data-modifying actions, a quorum authorization policy specifies such actions that require additional approval.

      • Quorum authorization roles

        Quorum authorization roles in RSC provide structured management of approvals and permissions, ensuring appropriate reviews for system changes. The QAuth administrator role oversees quorum authorization activities. The QAuth approver role, which can be added to custom roles, allows users to approve specific policy changes.

      • Quorum authorization process

        The Quorum Authorization (QAuth) process secures actions related to data management and system configuration. This involves enabling QAuth, creating and associating QAuth policies and roles, requesting and reviewing actions, and optionally excluding service accounts to ensure automation scripts are not blocked.

      • Initial setup of quorum authorization

        The initial setup of Quorum Authorization (QAuth) involves identifying and configuring primary and backup QAuth administrators, ensuring they do not have conflicting privileges, and enabling the QAuth feature to manage secure user authentication and administrative requests.

        • Enabling quorum authorization

          Enable quorum authorization for your RSC domain to enforce the requirement of receiving approvals from a minimum number of users before allowing critical, data-modifying operations to proceed.

        • Updating quorum authorization settings

          You can optimize the management of quorum authorization requests using the quorum authorization settings. With the required privileges, update the quorum authorization settings, such as the time-to-live setting for requests with manually scheduled actions and the timeout setting to indicate the duration after which inactive requests expire.

        • Disabling quorum authorization

          Disable quorum authorization for your RSC domain to relax the requirement of obtaining additional approvals before allowing critical, data-modifying actions. Disabling quorum authorization cancels pending quorum authorization requests, deletes the configured policies and the QAuth Admin role, and removes the permission to approve or deny requests from the QAuth reviewer role.

      • Configuration of quorum authorization policies and roles

        Configuring quorum authorization policies and roles enforces comprehensive data governance, enabling RSC administrators to centrally control, customize, and secure data management and system configuration actions.

      • Quorum authorization requests and approvals

        When a requester attempts an action protected by a policy, RSC triggers a quorum authorization request. The requester cannot perform the action until the request is approved by a user with the QAuth approver role.

      • Retention lock and quorum authorization

        The retention lock feature helps organizations meet legal and compliance standards by providing Compliance and Governance modes, both requiring quorum authorization approvals for specific actions.

Enabling quorum authorization

Enable quorum authorization for your RSC domain to enforce the requirement of receiving approvals from a minimum number of users before allowing critical, data-modifying operations to proceed.

Before you begin

When you enable quorum authorization, RSC creates the QAuth Admin role and assigns the role to a user account of your choice. The user account must be a local user account. You can either choose an existing user account or add an account for a new user in RSC.
Note:
The local user account you select for the QAuth Admin role must not have a role with the manage user access permission in the global or the tenant organization.

About this task

Only a user account with the global administrator role can enable quorum authorization.

Procedure

  1. Log in to RSC.
  2. Click and select Settings.
    The Settings menu appears.
  3. Click Quorum Authorization and click Settings.
    The Quorum Authorization page appears.
  4. Click Enable.
    The Enable Quorum Authorization wizard appears.
  5. In Email Address, enter the email address of a local user account.
    The local user account identifies the user who will be assigned the QAuth Admin role and will be the first QAuth admin in the RSC domain. The user account must exist in RSC.
  6. Click Enable.
    The Enable Quorum Authorization wizard progresses to the next page.
  7. Choose whether to switch the quorum authorization settings from your CDM-managed clusters to RSC.
    OptionDescription
    Yes To download a settings report and switch the settings, select Yes and click Download. RSC begins the switch and begins preparing the report. You can download the report from the File Download Center. RSC sends an email to the user account who is assigned the QAuth Admin role.
    No To choose not to download the settings report and switch the settings, select No.
    Rubrik recommends that you choose Yes to download the quorum authorization settings report for your CDM-managed clusters and to switch the configurations to RSC. This choice preserves a record of your settings and makes them available in RSC.
  8. Click Done.

Results

You have enabled quorum authorization in your RSC domain.

What to do next

Consider granting additional users the QAuth Admin role. Rubrik recommends having at least two users with the QAuth Admin role.

Create a quorum authorization policy to enforce quorum approval on actions you want to protect, as described in Creating a quorum authorization policy for system configuration actions.
