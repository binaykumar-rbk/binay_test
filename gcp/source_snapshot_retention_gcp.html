Source snapshot retention for GCP workloads

Source snapshot retention for GCP workloads ensures data protection and recoverability by allowing you to set various retention policies. These policies include retaining hourly, daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, and yearly snapshots to meet compliance, business continuity, and disaster recovery requirements.

Source snapshot retention refers to the length of time that RSC retains the source snapshots of your workload in GCP. This retention configuration is essential for ensuring that your workload is protected and recoverable within your specified policies.

You can set various retention policies to meet compliance, business continuity, and disaster recovery requirements.
Snapshot type Retention period Use case
Hourly snapshots 24 to 72 hours High-change environments: Captures frequent changes for applications with high transaction rates.
Daily snapshots 14 to 30 days Operational recovery: Quick recovery of recent changes due to accidental deletions or corruption.
Weekly snapshots 1 to 6 months Mid-term recovery: Balances frequent recovery needs with storage efficiency for less critical data.
Monthly snapshots 1 to 3 years Regulatory compliance: Meets legal or industry requirements for data retention and audits.
Quarterly snapshots 1 to 5 years Business continuity: Ensures prolonged data retention for quarterly reviews and audits.
Yearly snapshots More than 3 years Long-term archival: Preserves critical data for historical reference or long-term compliance needs.
