Primary snapshot retention for Azure DevOps

Primary snapshot retention for Azure DevOps organizations ensures data protection and recoverability by enabling you to set various retention policies, such as retaining hourly, daily, monthly, and yearly snapshots, to meet compliance, business continuity, and disaster recovery requirements.

Primary snapshot retention for Azure DevOps repositories defines how long RSC keeps the primary snapshots of your Git code within Azure. This retention configuration is important for ensuring that your repositories are protected and recoverable according to your business continuity and compliance policies.

You define your retention policies within an SLA Domain, which you then assign at the organization, project, or repository level in Azure DevOps. By setting different retention periods, you can meet compliance and rapid recovery needs.

Primary backup retention refers to the length of time that RSC retains the backup data bundle derived from the primary snapshot of your Azure DevOps organization. This bundle is stored in Azure Blob Storage. This retention configuration is essential for ensuring that your workload is protected and recoverable within your specified policies.

You can set various retention policies to meet compliance, business continuity, and disaster recovery requirements.

Snapshot type Retention period Use case
Hourly snapshots 24 to 72 hours High-change environments: Captures frequent changes for applications with high transaction rates.
Daily snapshots 14 to 30 days Operational recovery: Quick recovery of recent changes due to accidental deletions or corruption.
Monthly snapshots 1 to 3 years Regulatory compliance: Meets legal or industry requirements for data retention and audits.
Yearly snapshots More than 3 years Long-term retention: Preserves critical data for historical reference or long-term compliance needs.
Note:
RSC creates snapshots according to the defined schedule and retention policies, ensuring you have the necessary recovery points. When defining your policies, it is important to note that the minimum frequency for an Azure DevOps SLA Domain assignment is currently set to 8 hours.
